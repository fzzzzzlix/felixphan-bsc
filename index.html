Case 01  ·  Hero Case

TRESemmé Vietnam Consumer Insights Report and Media Plan Proposal in July, 2024

View Full Work ↗
Academic coursework project  ·  Role: Researcher and Media Planner

What this project is: In July 2024, TRESemmé Vietnam had strong TikTok performance but a declining Facebook audience and a near-zero Instagram presence. The numbers across platforms were not talking to each other. This project aimed to find out why. It was conducted in two phases: a consumer media insights report (analyzing TRESemmé's performance and positioning versus competitors Tsubaki and L'Oréal Professionnel across Facebook, TikTok, Instagram, and YouTube using Fanpage Karma) followed by a media plan proposal built directly from what the data revealed. All data was pulled manually; no third-party market reports were used.

Before: what the data showed

The numbers were not adding up

TRESemmé holds 2.1% market share in a premium haircare segment worth 5.2%. On paper, it should be growing. The platform data said otherwise.

The contradiction: TRESemmé dominated TikTok, 60k fans, 3.3% post interaction rate, 16% follower growth. At the same time, Facebook was contracting, Instagram was flatline, YouTube was losing ground. Momentum on one platform, contraction on every other.

Tsubaki had 26k TikTok fans but was growing on Facebook at +0.52% while TRESemmé contracted. L'Oréal had 6.5M Facebook followers and a 0.00% interaction rate: the opposite problem, but both revealed the same pattern. Scale without connection.

52% of Vietnamese consumers will not try a new brand unless a friend confirms it. TRESemmé had reach. It did not have the trust transfer to go with it.

After: from insights to media plan proposal

From Insights to Media Plan Proposal

"Step up your hair game at home."

The creative territory: not salon-grade product specs, but salon-grade confidence. The shift was about who plays the hero: not the product, but the woman using it. The Salon Credential strategy gave that shift a structure.

Case 02

Maggi Recipe Solution: Competitive and Consumers Analysis in July 2025, Vietnam

View Full Work ↗
Academic coursework, individual project  ·  Role: Researcher and Strategist

What this project is: Maggi Recipe Solution is a newer product line by Nestlé Vietnam: pre-mixed cooking bases designed to reduce meal prep complexity. Despite Maggi's 90%+ household penetration in Vietnam, the Recipe Solution line was struggling to build attention or loyalty. This project analyzed Maggi's brand communication on Facebook, TikTok, and YouTube against two direct competitors (Barona and Cholimex) over three months. The methodology included social listening via Fanpage Karma, sentiment analysis of 755 comments coded in NVivo, thematic analysis of 82 product reviews across Shopee, Lazada, Tiki, and BachHoaXanh, and a full customer journey map. The goal: find out what the brand was saying, what its audience was actually hearing, and what the gap between the two meant for strategy.

Before: the gap nobody was naming

Two keyword clouds. Different worlds.

Social listening across Facebook, TikTok, and YouTube, 74 posts and 755 comments coded in NVivo. The contrast between brands was immediate.

Maggi: top keywords
cao cap (premium) gia (price) chuan (standard) vi thom (aroma) Shopee
Barona: top keywords
de (easy) ngon (delicious) kham pha (explore) cong thuc (recipe) nha (home)

What the data revealed: Maggi's audience talked about the product in transactional terms: price, delivery speed, discount codes. Barona's audience talked about what they cooked with it. One brand was a purchase. The other was an experience.

364
Maggi FB comments
73
Positive comments only

Most commented brand of the three. Least loved. Awareness without affinity: the most common way a heritage brand loses its ground quietly.

Maggi was caught in a strategic blind spot: positioned too vaguely to command its premium pricing, priced too high to compete on value. Not a product problem. A narrative problem.

After: the strategy direction

Authentic Convenience

The repositioning territory: trusted taste, minimal effort, rooted in Vietnamese kitchen culture. A narrative shift, not a product upgrade.

1-step to tradition campaign #MaggiNhanhMaNgon Try Me Trust Me

85% of Vietnamese consumers say food is their main cultural anchor. Maggi has been present in over 90% of Vietnamese households for decades. The brand did not need a new product story. It needed to reconnect its existing story to what people actually care about when they cook.

2-tier influencer strategy

  • Tier 1, Awareness: TikTok mega-creators (Ha Cooking, 1.9M followers, 51.9M likes) lead branded hashtag challenges. 60-second meals. Real kitchens.
  • Tier 2, Trust: Facebook niche admins (Hoc Nau An, 3.6M followers) run live Q&As, recipe clinics, and ingredient transparency content.
  • Closing the loop: All influencer content links back to a centralized campaign, turning inspiration into consideration and purchase.
Authentic convenience positioning Community-first distribution Recipe remix challenges Ingredient transparency content
Summary 755 Facebook comments decoded to find one gap: MAGGI was competing on product attributes while Barona's audience was busy talking about what they cooked with it.
Case 03

Vinamilk Brand Bonding Investigation in 2023

View Full Proposal ↗
Academic coursework, individual project  ·  Research design proposal only, study not executed

What this project is: Vinamilk holds 43.7% of Vietnam's dairy market and is ranked the world's sixth-largest dairy brand. Despite that scale, the brand had no owned or monitored community on Facebook, its most important relationship-building platform. Dozens of fan groups existed, but the brand was not present in any of them. Nearly 48.5% of Vinamilk's Facebook audience reported feeling an information gap. This project proposed a research design to investigate Vinamilk's brand bonding on Facebook: specifically, how emotionally connected consumers were to the brand, and what factors drove or limited their involvement. The proposal covered target population, sampling design, full interview protocol, and two innovative techniques. The study was designed but not carried out; this is the methodology proposal.

Research Design Showcase

The marketing challenge

Facebook is the most effective platform for building brand-community relationships through group dynamics, but Vinamilk's existing groups operated without brand involvement: consumer-to-consumer only, no brand steering, no loyalty feedback loop. That absence, combined with a documented information gap among followers, was weakening brand bonding at scale.

The proposed study: 30 in-depth interviews across Hanoi, Ho Chi Minh City, and Da Nang, 10 per city, targeting Vietnamese Vinamilk consumers aged 25-44 who had joined and participated in Vinamilk-adjacent Facebook groups. Purposive sampling. In-person at cafes in each city.

Why in-depth interview?

Brand bonding involves emotional connectedness and brand involvement: two variables that surface through conversation, not through survey checkboxes. IDIs capture the why behind behavior, including the kind of associations people hold but would not volunteer unprompted.

Two innovative techniques in the protocol

Question 7, Projective Technique

Imaginative Scenario

Participants respond to a hypothetical scenario asking how Vinamilk relates to their self-image. The projective format bypasses social desirability bias and surfaces emotional associations that would not emerge from direct questioning. Designed to measure emotional connectedness.

Question 18, Visual Aid

Brand Interaction Screenshots

Participants are shown 5 real screenshots of Vinamilk's recent replies to customers on its Facebook page. Research confirms consumer impressions of brand interaction are more accurate when grounded in visual evidence than when recalled from memory alone.

The design principle: Do not ask people what they think of the brand. Show them something real, then ask how they feel. The gap between their reaction and the brand's intent is where the insight lives.

Note: research design only. No fieldwork was conducted as part of this coursework project.
Case 04  ·  Real Work
RMIT Vietnam Student Council Hanoi  ·  April 3-5, 2024

RMIT "Little Me" Exhibition Spring 2024

View Event Report ↗

A 3-day interactive mental wellness exhibition at RMIT Hanoi that I planned and ran as Head Organizer. The idea was simple: give students a space to slow down during exam season without making it feel like a therapy session.

Head Organizer

The planning story

What this event is: An interactive exhibition open to all students and staff at RMIT Hanoi, designed to make mental health conversations accessible during a high-stress semester. Collaborators included RMIT's Wellbeing Department (providing therapeutic content rigor), Current Media (earned coverage), and Artist My Tam (adding cultural reach and credibility).

The insight

Gen Z students at RMIT did not avoid mental health conversations because they did not care. They avoided them because the format usually felt like a formal intervention. The question was not what to say, but how to design an entry point that felt low-stakes enough to walk through.

The format

An exhibition, not a talk. Interactive stations people could move through at their own pace: Draw Little Me (sketch your younger self), DIY Calm Jar (sensory decompression activity), and a shared gallery of growth moments contributed by the RMIT community.
